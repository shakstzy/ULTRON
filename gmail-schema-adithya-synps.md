## 5. Content-based heuristics

Each as **if … then … because …**:

- **If** sender misspells the recipient's company name (e.g., "Synps" instead of "Synapse"), **then** mark as cold-spam, **because** real outreach researches the recipient and mass-merge templates don't catch typos.
- **If** body uses one-sentence-per-line copywriting structure (every line stands alone with a blank between), **then** likely a sales-template cold pitch, **because** that's the pattern trained by sales-email courses for "scannability."
- **If** sender domain is a throwaway TLD (`.info`, `.live`, `.site`, `.top`, `.services`), **then** ~95% likely cold-spam, **because** legitimate firms own brand `.com` domains and don't sign outreach from cheap TLDs.
- **If** sender domain is `<firstname>+<keyword>.com` (e.g., `campbellaihiring.com`, `peakridgecleaning.co`), **then** likely a single-person agency cold-pitch, **because** legitimate firms have multi-letter brand names not founder+function compounds.
- **If** body claims a volume metric ("Helped 300+ engineers", "10x faster", "$10M placed"), **then** sales agency, **because** real recruiters/VCs pitching one specific role don't lead with placement counts.
- **If** subject ends with a single-word CTA ("Yes?", "Worth a chat?", "Open to it?"), **then** template forcing-function, **because** real outreach proposes a specific time, candidate, or link.
- **If** subject starts with `[<bracket-tag>]` from a known operational vendor (Workspace, GCP, AI Studio), **then** likely an operational requirement (KEEP), **because** vendors use brackets for action mail to differentiate from marketing.
- **If** subject contains "Synapse" the company name, **then** route to `Synapse` label, **because** anything specifically referencing the startup is operational.
- **If** thread has multiple `Re:` prefixes with named participants and varying body text, **then** real conversation (preserve), **because** automated mail rarely chains.
- **If** sender pattern is `failed-payments+<account-id>@stripe.com`, **then** real billing alert (KEEP under Financial), **because** Stripe routes legitimate failed-payment notifications via that specific local-part.
- **If** vendor's noreply address sends both transactional AND marketing (e.g., `workspace-noreply@google.com`, `googleaistudio-noreply@google.com`), **then** scope by subject — keep `[Action *]` / `[Admin alert]` / `[Billing Update]` / "Domain registration" / "security", trash everything else, **because** the same address is reused for both purposes.
- **If** sender is `*@cluely.com` and body is a "From the CEO" / product-launch update, **then** mass-blast (trash), **because** founder updates from non-portfolio companies are templated marketing even when signed by the founder.
- **If** sender is `inceptionprogram@nvidia.com` (NOT `news@nvidia.com`), **then** real program correspondence (KEEP under `Programs`), **because** NVIDIA Inception uses a dedicated local-part for accepted-startup comms.
- **If** Drive share subject contains `Veera` or `Meridian`, **then** route to `Veera`, **because** that's the side-advisory work (separate from Synapse).
- **If** Drive share subject contains `Lease` / `Landlord` / `Rent`, **then** route to `Personal`, **because** those are real-estate docs not startup work.
- **If** mail is from a calendar-invite subject prefix (`Invitation:`, `Reminder:`, `Accepted:`, etc.), **then** trash regardless of sender, **because** the meeting outlives the reminder noise.
- **If** body has no personalization to Synapse (no mention of MCP, crypto, registry, what the company actually does), **then** likely template, **because** real outreach references something specific about the recipient's work.
- **If** attachment is a PDF named like an invoice or contract (e.g., `5552317754.pdf` from Google Payments), **then** keep under `Financial`, **because** that's the audit trail for billing.
- Body-length heuristic isn't reliable on this account — both real emails and spam vary widely. Don't filter by length.
- CC/BCC patterns aren't observed enough on this account (mostly 1:1 outreach) to drive rules.
