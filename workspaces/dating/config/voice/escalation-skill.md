# Escalation Skill

Canonical funnel patterns for moving a Tinder match to a locked first date. Read by stages 03 (Layer A), 04 (Layers B and C), and 05 (Layer D).

Synthesized from `wiki/raw/research/tinder-escalation-skill.md` (Reddit 2024-2026 consensus + Hinge Labs, Zhang & Yasseri). Keep this file under 200 lines; defer raw evidence to the source doc.

**Core thesis.** Move faster than feels natural, be more specific than feels polite, do not pen-pal.

**Hard caps** (see `funnel-rules.md`): number ask by message 7 or day 4, whichever hits first. Default date venues: coffee, boba, walk.

---

## Layer A: Mid-Conversation (stage 03)

Goal: build enough shared context that a specific date proposal feels natural. Target window 3-8 back-and-forths over 24-72 hours.

### A1. Mirror-length rule
Match her message length and emoji density within one sentence and one emoji. Break only to land a punchline (shorter) or share a personal beat that invites reciprocity (slightly longer). **Apply every turn.**

### A2. Shared-niche anchor
By message 3-5, identify one specific thing from her profile or early messages (coffee shop, band, hike, food) and anchor the chat to it. This anchor should later become the date venue. **Use once, early.**

### A3. Ask-share-tease rotation
Roughly 1:1:1. Ask one specific question, share one short personal beat in response, tease lightly every third or fourth message. Stacking three questions in a row reads as a job interview. **Use after the first 2 exchanges.**

### A4. Callback loop
Pick one small detail from her first one or two messages (a typo, a weirdly strong opinion, a hobby) and re-reference it playfully every 4-6 messages. Manufactures inside-joke energy before any exists. **Activate the moment a callback-worthy detail appears.**

### A5. Move-toward topics
Steer toward: weekend plans, what she is obsessed with right now, Austin food/drink favorites, a local event. Steer away from: abstract work talk, news, heavy philosophy, how-was-your-day loops. **Pivot into a move-toward topic by message 4 at the latest.**

### A6. Receptivity Checklist (gates the close in Layer B)
Before close, confirm at least **3 of 5**:

1. She is asking questions back.
2. Her messages match or exceed yours in length.
3. Reply times under 1 hour during waking hours.
4. Emojis or exclamation points are warming.
5. She volunteered a personal detail unprompted.

3 or more -> high-probability close. 2 or fewer -> either force close under cap anyway or let match fade.

## Layer B: Number Close (stage 04)

Goal: the number, bundled as logistics for a specific date proposal. A number-first ask reads transactional. A plan-first ask bundles the number as logistics and converts far higher.

### B1. Plan-first, number-as-logistics *(default)*
Propose the specific date first (venue, rough day, short duration). Once she accepts or counter-proposes, ask for the number as an afterthought.
Shape: `There is a good boba spot near South Congress. Want to grab one Saturday afternoon? Easier to coordinate over text, what is your number?`

### B2. Off-app convenience frame
Attribute the move off-app to Tinder's bad notifications, not to escalation.
Shape: `Tinder eats half my notifications. Here is mine if you want to just text: [number].` Offering yours first reduces her perceived risk.

### B3. Announcement, not question
Phrase the ask as statement of intent, not permission request. "Shoot me your number and I will send the spot" outperforms "can I get your number." **Only use once 3+ A6 signals are present.**

### B4. Give first to get
Drop your number inside the plan message.
Shape: `Saturday 3 at [place] works? My number is [number], text me so I have yours.` Good default for matches that seem interested but careful.

### B5. Signal-triggered vs cap-triggered close
Signal-triggered (closing at the first conversational peak after 3+ A6 signals) outperforms cap-triggered because it feels natural. Cap-triggered (message 7 or day 4 regardless) outperforms waiting past the cap. **Rule: signal if you can, cap if you must. Never let cap elapse without an attempt.**

### B6. Low-stakes venue naming
Always pair the number ask with a specific venue. "Coffee at Greater Goods Thursday evening" converts far better than "let's grab a drink sometime." **Always pair with any close.**

### Dodge-recovery (Layer B)
If she says she does not give out her number, or goes silent after the ask:

1. **De-escalate in one line.** "Totally fair, no pressure." Zero defensiveness.
2. **Switch to in-app date.** "We can lock the plan here. Saturday 3 at [venue]?"
3. **Offer yours only.** "No worries, here is mine if you want: [number]. Either way, [venue] Saturday?"
4. **Silent 24-48 hrs.** Send one light message tied to an earlier A4 callback. Do not re-ask.
5. **Silent past 72 hrs.** One final low-effort pivot then move on.
6. **Three-strike rule.** Dodge + dodge-in-app + cold-on-pivot -> deprioritize.

### Layer B anti-patterns
Asking in first 1-3 messages with zero rapport. Asking with no plan attached. Pushing Snapchat or Instagram as first off-app ask (reads catfish or hookup). Escalating right after one good joke (let 2-3 beats compound). Double-texting the number request after silence.

## Layer C: Date Proposal (stage 04, continued)

Goal: lock a specific date 2-5 days out. "We should hang out sometime" is the number-one proposal-failure mode.

### C1. Specific single-shot *(default when signal is clear)*
One venue, one day, one time window.
Shape: `Greater Goods Thursday around 6?`

### C2. Constrained A/B
Two options on one axis only (never vary both). "Thursday or Saturday?" at a named spot, or one day at two venues. 3+ options read indecisive. **Use when her schedule is unclear but her interest is clear.**

### C3. Activity-anchored ask
Tie the proposal directly to something she said (A2 anchor). "You mentioned you haven't tried [boba spot]. Thursday 7?" **Use when an A2 anchor exists.**

### C4. Soft-frame bridge
Propose in principle first, lock logistics after she agrees. "We should actually do this. You around later this week?" -> "cool, Mozart's Thursday 6?" **Use when banter is still warming and hard ask feels premature.**

### C5. Bounded window
Name a 2-3 day window instead of a specific slot. "I'm around Wed or Thu evening, which works?" **Fallback when you want specificity without picking a slot she's booked on.**

### Austin venue reasoning
Coffee / boba / walk as defaults because: heat (95-105F May-Sept rules out long outdoor dinners), cohort (young + tech + grad-student-heavy = 45-min coffee > 2-hour dinner on a first meet), Lady Bird + Zilker are central and photogenic, daytime-sober reads "wants to know me" not "wants to sleep with me," boba is normalized non-alcoholic option in Austin's Asian + tech scene. Specific venues live in `shared/date-venues.md`.

### Dodge-recovery (Layer C)
Signal is whether she volunteers a concrete alternative within 24 hours, not whether she replies.

- **"Let me check my schedule."** One forward-moving ping at 24h ("all good, if this week is crazy, how is Tuesday next week?"). One only.
- **"I'm busy this week."** Counter with specific slot on the far side ("makes sense, Tuesday or Saturday next week?"). Converts deflection into binary.
- **Second vague response.** Drop ask. Send one light non-date message 2-3 days later. Re-propose once if she reengages warmly. Otherwise move on.
- Never: ask why, send paragraphs, sexualize to rescue. Graceful fallback: "no worries, hit me up when things clear up."

## Layer D: Post-Number iMessage (stage 05)

Goal: keep warmth alive until the date. Not build more rapport. The 45-message marathon that creates false intimacy and then collapses in-person is the top failure mode here.

### Voice-shift principles
- **Shorter.** 1 sentence or a fragment, not 1-3.
- **More casual, more lowercase.** Over-punctuation and formal capitalization kill the vibe.
- **Fewer emojis.** One per several messages. Over-emoji reads try-hard.
- **Lower volume.** 2-6 messages per day max before the date.
- **Forward-only.** Do not rehash Tinder topics. Move to details about your day, the plan, or a small tease.
- **Match her timing within 2-3x.** Instant replies when she takes 3 hours read over-eager. 24h waits read transparent.

### Day-of confirmation
Timing: morning-of for evening date; night-before for pre-noon date.
Shape: `[greeting fragment] + [confirm time] + [confirm venue] + [one small specific detail or light tease]`
Example: `hey, still good for 6 at Greater Goods? heads up parking on the north side is easier.`
Avoid: "looking forward to it" (formal and needy), "you still down?" (invites the out).

### Flake and cancel handling
- **Cancels with reason + new day.** Lock new day immediately. ~70% still happen.
- **Cancels with reason, no new day.** Reply short and light. Propose alternative 24-48h later. Vague again -> stop.
- **Day-of cancel.** Brief non-punishing reply. Let it breathe a day before rescheduling.
- **No-show, no text.** One message that night or next morning ("hope everything's okay"). Require locked time in that same conversation if she wants to reschedule.
- **You need to reschedule.** Apologize once, name reason briefly, propose 2 specific alternatives in the same message.

**Default rule: one chase, one recovery attempt, then disengage.**

### Behaviors correlated with no-shows (avoid)
Over-texting (20+ per day). Double-texting within hours of no reply. Heavy emotional topics pre-meet (exes, childhood, mental health). Sexual escalation pre-meet. Long paragraphs. Scheduling more than 7 days out. Switching venue more than once. Asking "are we still on?" more than once.

## Signal Catalog

### Green (escalate)
Asks follow-up questions. Uses your name. Message length matches or exceeds yours. Reply times under 2 hrs during waking hours. Initiates a new topic after a gap. Teases or banters back. Volunteers her schedule. Sends a photo, meme, or voice note. Emojis warm up. Asks what you're doing this weekend.

### Yellow (hold / gentle push)
One-word replies but still responding within a day. Reply cadence drifting hours to half-day. Neutral emojis without words. Answers your questions but asks none back. Deflects personal questions with humor.

### Red (deprioritize)
Dry one-word replies over 3+ turns. 24+ hour silences with no context. Zero questions returned over 4+ of her messages. Vague on specific plans. "I'm really busy right now" with no alternative. "I have a lot going on." Stops opening messages.

## Profile-Specific Calibration for Adithya

**Trap: credential-stacking.** Berkeley + Stanford GSB + GP + VC fund + ex-crypto reads status-anxious to thoughtful career-oriented women. Pick one credential signal at most for any messaging context. "Based in Austin, investing in AI agents, at Stanford for grad school" is enough. **Actively suppress ex-crypto.**

**Edge: athleticism + Austin tech-adjacent grad-student cohort.** 5'10 athletic at 23 in Austin is median, not outlier.

**Voice modes to avoid:**
- GSB-polish (reads interview).
- VC-bro (reads trying to close a round).

**Voice mode to default to:** founder-hustle + intellectually curious (playful about a hard problem, weekend project energy, genuine specific take).

**Discipline that matters most for this profile:** under-invest in messaging volume, over-invest in plan specificity.
