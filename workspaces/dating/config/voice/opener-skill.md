# Opener Skill

Guidelines for drafting openers on a brand-new Tinder match. Read by stage 03 when thread state is `opener-needed`.

Canonical patterns synthesized from `wiki/raw/research/tinder-opener-skill.md` (Reddit 2024-2026 consensus + OkCupid, Hinge Labs, Zhang & Yasseri, Dai & Robbins, Irrational Labs). Keep this file under 200 lines; defer raw evidence to the source doc.

---

## Principles

1. **Specificity beats cleverness.** Reference one concrete, non-appearance detail from her profile. "You mention" correlates with roughly 50 percent reply lift (OkCupid); Hinge prompt comments convert 47 percent more often than photo likes.
2. **Every opener carries one easy, specific question.** Statement-only openers die. "How's it going" beats "hey." "What's up" beats "hi."
3. **Length: 1 to 2 sentences, 40 to 90 characters.** Paragraphs signal try-hard. One-word messages signal lazy. Median winning opener is around 8 words.
4. **Humor helps, never paired with a looks compliment.** Funny openers outperform earnest by 12 points (Irrational Labs). Humor-plus-appearance backfires (Dai & Robbins).
5. **No compliment on looks, ever.** Taste compliments (books, music, travel, cooking) are fine because they prove you read the profile.
6. **Send within hours of matching, not days.** Median first message lands within 8 hours of match. Fast replies read positive; "playing it cool" buries you in her feed.
7. **Match her tone and energy.** Playful bio earns playful opener. Earnest bio earns earnest opener. Mismatch reads auto-generated.
8. **No phone number, Snapchat, Instagram, or sex in message 1.** Number exchange median is message 27, not 1.

## Opener Structures

### 2.1 Specific Detail Hook *(default)*
Shape: `[One concrete non-appearance observation from her bio or photo background] + [one open question tied to it].`
Use when: profile has any concrete content (book, trail, dish, band, travel location, prompt answer).
Do not use when: profile is truly empty or all near-identical selfies.

### 2.2 Callback Contradiction
Shape: `Your [element A] says X but your [element B] shows Y. [Playful demand for the story].`
Use when: profile has enough content to find two elements in gentle tension (e.g., "introvert" bio + group photos).
Do not use when: bio is thin, or the "contradiction" is body-related.

### 2.3 Niche Insider Reference
Shape: `Fellow [shared niche] here. [Low-stakes taste question inside that niche].`
Use when: she signals a specific subculture you *genuinely* know (film camera, climbing gym, specific band, anime).
Do not use when: you couldn't actually hold the conversation. Fake interest gets clocked by message 3.

### 2.4 Playful Hypothetical
Shape: `[Light absurd premise tied to her profile] + [forced-choice or tiny question].`
Use when: her bio or prompts signal humor or absurd tone.
Do not use when: profile is serious or intent-heavy; also skip if the hypothetical is copy-paste canon ("would you rather zombie apocalypse" is cooked).

### 2.5 Two Truths and a Lie
Shape: `Game framing + three short claims about yourself (one false) + invitation to guess and reciprocate.`
Use when: you have one genuinely weird or specific claim. Branches into three conversation threads in one message.
Do not use when: all three claims are bland.

### 2.6 Recommendation Ask
Shape: `You look like you'd actually know [specific domain from her profile]. Give me your pick for [narrow, answerable request].`
Use when: profile shows taste or expertise (food, travel, music, a local Austin venue).
Do not use when: the ask is generic ("recommend me a book").

### 2.7 Light Self-Deprecating Curiosity
Shape: `Small, true, low-stakes admission about yourself + specific question about her that gives her room to one-up or reciprocate.`
Use when: you want to soften a credentials-heavy profile so you do not read as a flex. *Important for this user.*
Do not use when: the self-deprecation targets anything physical, financial, or sounds like fishing.

### 2.8 Prompt Callback
Shape: `Quote her prompt or bio line + react + ask the follow-up she clearly wanted.`
Use when: she wrote a prompt or bio line that is clearly a hook.
Do not use when: she has no text; or the line is values-heavy and the callback would be a joke.

### 2.9 Front-Loaded Soft Date Invitation
Shape: `One specific observation + a concrete low-pressure plan tied to it + an easy question.`
Use when: her intent reads "open to meeting" (recent Austin photos, travels-to-Austin prompt, partner-in-crime prompts) and she is 21+.
Do not use when: she signals "long-term only" or "not sure" in profile intent. Avoid as opener on LTR profiles; hold for message 2 or 3.

### 2.10 Meta-Acknowledgement *(empty-bio rescue)*
Shape: `Cheerful meta-note that her profile is sparse + one fun answerable question unrelated to her looks.`
Use when: bio is empty and photos give you nothing.
Do not use when: she does have referenceable content. Using this then signals you didn't look.

## Decision Tree

- **Bio has 2+ concrete details?** -> **2.1** default.
  - Two details create tension -> **2.2**.
  - Detail is a niche you genuinely know -> **2.3**.
  - Tone is playful/absurd -> **2.4**.
  - Taste or expertise visible -> **2.6**.
- **Bio has 1 line only** -> **2.8** quoting that line, or **2.1** off a photo background.
- **No bio text** -> photos.
  - Non-selfie activity visible (climbing, travel, cooking, art) -> **2.1** on the activity.
  - Only selfies in same setting -> **2.10** or **2.7**.
- **Stated intent overrides:**
  - Long-term / relationship -> lean **2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 2.7**. Avoid 2.9 in opener. 2-3 exchanges before date ask.
  - Not sure / open -> **2.4, 2.5, 2.6** play well. Date ask after 3-5 exchanges.
  - Short-term / casual -> any of 2.1-2.7, then **2.9** as message 2 or 3.
- **Photo overrides:**
  - Pet prominently featured -> **2.1** on the pet, never on her body.
  - Recent local Austin landmark -> **2.6** or **2.9**.
  - Travel photo -> ask about the trip, not the outfit.
- **Fallback if first opener got no reply in 48 hours:** do not send a second text. Follow-ups after silence lower eventual reply probability. Move on.

## Profile-Specific Calibration for Adithya

The Tinder profile does the flexing (Berkeley EECS + Haas, Stanford GSB, GP title, athletic build). The opener's job is therefore the opposite: prove he actually reads her and can hold a light conversation. **Highest-leverage structures: 2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 2.7.** 2.7 is especially important because credential-leading reads as red flag to the target cohort.

Guardrails:

- Do not mention gym, fitness, protein, or lifting in the opener. Reads try-hard flex when paired with visible gym photos.
- Do not mention crypto, AI agents, or VC unless she brings it first. Save Outer Scope Ventures for in-person.
- Match energy to her profile tone: playful creative types (SXSW, music, design) -> 2.4 or 2.1; UT / startup-adjacent women -> 2.3 or 2.8; quieter earnest profiles -> 2.6 or 2.1 without humor.
- Send window: 6pm to midnight within 2 hours of match (peak Tinder activity).

## Anti-Patterns

1. "Hey" / "Hi" / "Hello" / "Sup" -> worst-performing openers on record.
2. "How's your day going?" / "How are you?" -> forces her to invent all content.
3. Compliments on face or body.
4. Name puns or recycled pickup lines.
5. Sexual openers or genitalia references.
6. "Why are you still single?"
7. "Tell me about yourself."
8. Copy-paste memes or Reddit screenshots as openers.
9. Negs and backhanded compliments.
10. Multi-paragraph essay openers.
11. Requesting phone, Snap, or Instagram in message 1.
12. Pet names before meeting ("hey babe," "hey beautiful," "baby").
