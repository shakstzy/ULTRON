## 13:47 — sydney

just chatted with this guy https://x.com/tylvuong17/status/2026113664491606017?s=20
some insights
• he started the voice team at scale mid last year because they were getting a lot of audio data requests
• he said ai labs really struggle with voice evals as the performance is not as straightforward as text since there are so many more dimensions like emotions, cadence, slang and it's not just the content of what's being said
    ◦ people also interpret speech differently based on their own location/background
• one thing he's thinking about is context of the speaker, some examples:
    ◦ a human customer service agent naturally adjusts how they speak depending on the environment of the person they’re talking to - if the customer is outside with significant background noise, the agent may slow their pace and project more clearly than they would with someone speaking from a quiet room
    ◦ drive thru voice agent - how does a drive thru agent decipher what's part of the order vs side conversations in the car
    ◦ adults take on a different tone with kids/babies - a voice ai needs to recognize they're talking to a child and change how they respond
