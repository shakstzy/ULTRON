# #app-team — 2026-03-22 (Sunday)

## 18:48 — Adithya Kumar (me)

a productive week at gtc, sent out a ton of follow ups, In general, massive learnings from a repositioning angle as to where we sit in the stack and how we're going to sell data to who. The week also shed a ton of light on the types of data that people are prioritizing. It's readily obvious now that people really care about high-quality data rather than massive corpuses of data. Aside from one of my conversations with a guy who founded Bones and the RP1 data set, Nvidia, Google Cloud., Meta, and every other major lab is a regular customer, not due to their volume of the corpus of data they have, they only have 800 hours, but due to the fidelity of data they'e collected.

The other major learning is most major data labs either have in-house labeling already, namely Deepgram and Eleven, and only need help ascertaining super long-tail assets. From this week, I've gathered that at least for voice audio, we should focus on dialects, as in non-native speakers., for which I've collected a couple contacts that we could tap into, and the Arabic Gulf region and Haitian Creole data sets. I actually met a guy. that specifically works with Haitian Creole people through a health organization that he runs. So hopefully we'll have some spikes to deliver there.

Also got the contact of a ton of Accelerators. The companies at GTC were honestly just too rich to be our customers, they're too far along the stack to start readily accepting very obvious corpuses of data like just natural conversational audio. There needs to be some X factor with data, is what I learned. Something that makes it hard to collect. These folks are already have teams on. scraping public data and public audio, so there's no need for us to be duplicating efforts there. I think the only reason that we would do that is to bolster our own claim to fame being like we have 50,000 or 100,000 hours of audio, etc., etc., which might be just a signal for some teams to be like we're a legitimate player in the space.
