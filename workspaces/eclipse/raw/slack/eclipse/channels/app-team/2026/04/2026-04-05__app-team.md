> re: audio normalization, if anyone is interested in comparing the ways different processing combination, [you can find it here](https://drive.google.com/drive/u/0/folders/1bGmWG2CYcBkpsx7ky93qg9mAxkltGjbN).  curious to hear other ppls opinions
>
> so theres 3 different processing flags each with their own set of settings we can configure. loudness is pretty set for this deal, but SNR and SRR will take some tweaking to find out what sounds best, and it will probably vary based on how rough the source audio is so we'll prob need to figure out a few good presets
>
> • normalize (required, fixed atm to meet client reqs)
> • noise reduction (SNR)
> • reverb reduction (SRR)
> review of the options available:
> • normalize - yep, makes it louder, not much else
> • normalize + SNR - TBD, had to fix this one a bunch due to a processing race condition that nearly made my laptop eat itself
> • normalize + SRR - this one was very noticable, fixed recordings with crazy reverb and made it feel way more contained while still feeling natural
> • normalize + SNR + SRR - felt unnatural, compared to just SRR, the SNR seemed to remove TOO much reverb, but that my 2c
> I want to play with lowering the SNR while keeping medium SRR next, think the noise reduction needs to be wrangled a bit more
>
> we will want to tweak what low/medium/high mean as we iterate and find what settings work best for us
