# #general — 2026-03-03 (Tuesday)

## 07:02 — Adithya Kumar (me)

Hey team — sharing some thoughts on where we should be focusing sales efforts based on the intel report + recent conversations.

We've been saying "sell to AI labs" but the report makes it clear there are really 4 distinct buyer profiles, each wanting different things:

:dart: **Highest priority right now:**

**1. Voice AI companies (ElevenLabs, Deepgram, Cartesia, Hume)**
Fastest path to revenue. 2-8 week sales cycles, mid-range deals ($10K-$200K), and they buy repeatedly. ElevenLabs literally has a Voice Data Partnerships page asking for exactly what we sell — human-to-human conversations with consent chains. Deepgram just raised $130M and committed to passing the "Audio Turing Test" in 2026. They need accent-diverse conversational audio badly, especially for retail/restaurant environments after acquiring OfOne.

Key data they want: natural conversations (not scripted), diverse accents/dialects, 44.1kHz+ sample rate, full provenance docs. Hume is interesting — they need emotionally labeled audio, a premium niche nobody else fills well.

**2. Frontier labs under litigation pressure (Anthropic, OpenAI)**
Anthropic just settled Bartz for $1.5B. OpenAI has preservation orders across multiple lawsuits. Legal teams now sit at the procurement table — consent-verified data isn't a nice-to-have, it's a compliance requirement. Longer cycles (3-6 months) but $100K-$1M+ deals. We already have relationships here so it's about expanding scope.

**3. Data brokers as a channel play ([Defined.ai](http://Defined.ai), Lionbridge)**
Instead of selling direct to every small lab, supply brokers who resell to their enterprise clients. Lower margin per unit but way less customer acquisition cost and higher volume.

**4. Vertical AI (medical, education, sales coaching)**
Smaller deals ($5K-$50K) but they'll pay premium for domain-expert recordings — doctors, lawyers, educators. Nuance/Microsoft Health, Abridge, Gong, Duolingo all need specialized conversational audio their generic providers can't source.

**What's changed in the market that helps us:**
• The litigation wave (Bartz, Vacker, Lehrman) created real urgency — compliance teams are blocking purchases of data without clean consent chains. That's our entire differentiator.
• Conversational AI market projected at $18B this year, heading to $82B by 2034.
• Competitors like Shaip, Appen, David AI sell audio data but none have our consent-verified provenance chain. Post-litigation, that gap matters.

**My recommendation for this week:**
• ElevenLabs first — actively soliciting, flush with cash, post-settlement. Submit through their partnerships page + warm LinkedIn outreach to their data team.
• Deepgram second — similar urgency, accent diversity is their stated gap.
• [Defined.ai](http://Defined.ai) for channel — one deal here multiplies across their client base.

We've been pricing at $35-40/hr for exclusive licensing. Market shows tier-2 data labs pay $60-65/hr, so we have margin room. For brokers, lower per-unit but make it up on volume.

Full report has LinkedIn search queries, conference targets (ICASSP in May is the big one), and company-by-company contact strategies if anyone wants to dig deeper.
