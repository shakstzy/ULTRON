# Virality Playbook

Operating doctrine for promoting Adithya's DistroKid releases on TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts. Encode every rule below into the content pipeline; revisit quarterly.

## Core thesis

Volume × surgical hook selection × algorithmic-burner rotation. The 2019 model (one polished MV + pray) is dead. The 2026 model is **2-tier hybrid**: 1 hero clip (paid-boost-eligible) per song + 30–100 burner-account variants flooding TikTok/Shorts/Reels in parallel to find the winning 7–15s window.

## The Wants-and-Needs transition format

**Anatomy.** 1.5–2.5s contextual setup → match-cut on the beat drop → 7–15s vocal-led music-video window. Setup steals attention from a non-music context (POV / "when X" / mundane scene). Match-cut creates a closure compulsion the brain must resolve. The audio reward arrives co-located with the cut, guaranteeing 3-second hold rate >60% which is the algorithmic threshold for 5–10× distribution.

**Hook duration: 1.5–2.5s, NOT 3s.** The old "3-second hook" advice is the outer bound. Drop must land before second 3. If music drop lands later than 02.5s, cut another half-second from the setup. If you can't, the setup concept is wrong.

**Match-cut variables (ranked by impact)**:
1. **Motion match** — whip pan, head turn, object thrown carries eye-tracking momentum through the cut (2001's bone→spaceship)
2. **Shape/composition match** — circle in setup → circle in MV first frame; brain perceptually fuses the two
3. **Color-flash match** — dominant red → dominant red; cheapest to engineer
4. **Eyeline match** — character looking screen-left → artist looking back from screen-right
5. **Audio bridge** — door slam / lighter click sample-replaced by song's first transient

**Concrete pair templates** (require at least one match per cut):
- `object trajectory → body movement` (thrown shirt → outfit reveal)
- `door / opening → scene reveal` (door swings → MV first scene)
- `fall / drop motion → beat drop` (object falls → drop hits frame)
- `camera whip → music-video camera whip` (whip-pan → whip-pan)
- `same color block across cut` (red wall → red lighting)

**Backwards snippet selection rule.** Pick the song's 7–15s window by what the corresponding music video's *first frame* looks like. Its dominant shape, color, motion vector dictates which setup clips can cut from. This is reverse from how indie artists usually pick snippets.

## Hook framing — the 4 setups that convert

1. **POV: you're [emotionally specific scenario]** — narrative hooks, +38% engagement vs generic
2. **When [universal feeling] hits and this song plays** — emotional-resonance, 60%+ of top music posts
3. **Mundane-to-dramatic** — walking to bodega → drop into chorus
4. **This song is for the person who [archetype]** — identification trigger, native to emo-rap audience

**Never** write "stream my new song." Write the *feeling* you want the listener to admit, then reverse-engineer a 2-second visual that triggers that admission.

## Vocal-led, NOT drop-led

7–15s windows starting **one bar before a sing-along lyrical hook**, ending after the most-quotable 1–2 lines. Drops can be the *cut point* from the setup, but the propagating sound must be vocal-led. Reasons: (a) viewers can't lip-sync a synth, (b) drops feel "loud" without context on phone speakers, (c) algorithm reads completion worse on drops because viewers feel rewarded *at* the drop and bounce. Indie Music Academy: vocal-hook lip-sync earns 40% higher participation than BTS content.

## Per-platform divergence (2026)

**TikTok** (sound-first discovery engine)
- Length: 9–21s for music; sound library audio bound at 60s default
- Hook: hit by 1.5s; 71% decide-to-keep-watching by second 3
- Hashtags: 3–5 max; algorithm treats caption + on-screen text + STT + hashtags as one signal cluster
- Posting: 1–4×/day per account official guidance; 3–5×/week the Buffer sweet spot
- Best window: **Tue–Thu 5–9 PM local** (sound-on listening); avoid Wed/Thu mornings, Saturdays
- Sound-reuse is the #1 viral signal

**Instagram Reels** (community-engagement engine)
- Length: 7–15s for high loop rate; up to 30s narrative-driven
- **Hashtags hard-capped at 5 since Dec 2025.** Excess gets stripped. Generic tags actively hurt reach
- Caption is now a search engine signal; write captions for SEARCH, not for hashtags
- Algorithm priority order: shares-via-DM > saves > comments > likes
- Best window: **Tue 1–7 PM, Wed 12–9 PM local** (Sprout 2026, ~2B engagements analyzed)
- Trial Reels (24-hr follower-only window) for pre-validation before public push

**YouTube Shorts** (compounding evergreen engine)
- Length: **35–60s outperforms <30s** — Shorts cross-promotes to long-form viewers, rewards retention
- The ONLY platform where evergreen content compounds. Shorts get views from search + sidebar months after upload — biggest reason a back-catalog artist must prioritize
- Title + description = SEO. Include song title, artist, genre, feeling keyword
- Audience skews older; less viral spike, more compound growth

**Posting mix for back-catalog revival artist: 50% TikTok burner volume / 30% Shorts (keyword-rich titles per song) / 20% Reels (highest-quality cuts only)**

## AI tool stack (May 2026)

**Defaults** (free / cheap):
- **Veo 3.1** — became free for all Google accounts April 2026 (10 clips/month). Cinematic photoreal + native audio. Indie default
- **Higgsfield Cinema Studio** — aggregator/preset platform; 11M users, 1.2B impressions since Sept 2025
- **Higgsfield Soul Cinematic / Nano Banana Pro** — image gen for reference frames

**Paid hero shots** (1 per song):
- **Sora 2 / Sora 2 Pro** — narrative consistency, longer (15s+), native synced audio
- **Kling 3.0 (Omni One)** — 4K 60fps, multi-shot storyboard, best physics

**For lip-sync of artist on AI character**:
- Hedra / Higgsfield Avatars / Veed lip-sync / OmniHuman

**Skip**: Runway Gen-4 (losing share, shallow audio integration)

**The AI-as-VFX-layer rule.** Do NOT generate the artist performing. Generate the *world around* an existing iPhone-shot of the artist (set extension, sky replacement, color-grade). Human anchor stays real. AI face replacement is in the AI-slop blast radius — Spotify removed 75M+ AI tracks in 12 months; "slopperator" is a fan insult now.

## Aesthetic hierarchy (melodic-rap / emo-rap)

1. **Dreamcore / liminal Y2K** — empty malls, hotel hallways, late-night gas stations, low-poly PS1 grain, CRT scanlines. Native visual language of emo-rap fans in 2026
2. **Cinematic photoreal "main character"** — rain-soaked streets, helicopter overheads, Tokyo neon (Higgsfield Cinema Studio presets, Sora 2 Pro)
3. **Y2K Futurism / VHS / camcorder grain** — Sabrina Carpenter, $UICIDEBOY$, PinkPantheress users; grain disguises generation artifacts
4. **Soft anime** — only when paired with genuinely emotional vocals; otherwise reads as slop

**Filter every AI clip**: "Could a fan tell this is AI in 1 second?" If yes, either lean ALL THE WAY into stylized (anime, low-poly, dreamcore) so AI-ness is a feature, OR replace with phone footage. Avoid the photoreal-uncanny-valley middle.

## Genre-specific format ranking (melodic-rap / emo-rap / pop)

1. **Lyric-typography overlay** synced to punchline (one-syllable explosions of text)
2. **Late-night driving footage** + slowed-reverb edit (Lil Peep / Juice WRLD legacy aesthetic)
3. **POV "you're in the car at 3 AM"** narrative hook → drop into chorus
4. **Performance lip-sync close-up** (eyes-only or lower-face only — "anonymous emo")
5. **Fan-reaction stitch** ("first listen reactions" — rare-but-explosive when seeded)

15–30s clips for emo-rap because lyrical specificity needs time to land. Too-short snippets reduce to vibes without payoff. Reference: sombr's "back to friends" — 7.7M video uses, most-saved on TikTok in 2025.

## Burner-page playbook

Will Morrow (Warner Records SVP) used 0-follower burner accounts to break sombr, Benson Boone, Teddy Swims. Range Media Partners posts 5,000–15,000 videos/day per artist client. Hundred Days runs Discord-based clipping communities paying creators per view.

**Indie-scale rule**: 1 main artist account + 3–5 themed burner pages. Each burner posts 2–4×/day. Burners use the song as sound, never directly self-promote artist by name. Themes: "sad night drives", "emo rap edits", "after-hours rap", "POV emotional moments", "3 AM thoughts".

This gives **15–40 daily algorithmic shots** without burning out the artist's main brand. Main account gets 1–2/day high-quality (face, performance, BTS, DM-the-fans).

**Hard ceilings**:
- No view-bot services (DistroKid forwards stream-fraud penalties; 60% of detected stream fraud per Spotify originates from social-promo tactics)
- Burners must look genuinely human (varied content, real engagement) — copy-paste networks get nuked in TikTok's coordinated-inauthentic purges
- Avoid CapCut for face-of-project content (ByteDance 2025 ToS update gave them broad AI-training rights to creator likeness); use Premiere/DaVinci/Final Cut for face content, reserve CapCut for faceless

## DistroKid sound-library mechanics

**Most common single mistake**: filming with audio playing through a speaker → TikTok mutes it as "original sound". Always select song from TikTok's sound library when posting; never record audio over phone speaker.

Required setup:
1. DistroKid → "TikTok & other ByteDance stores" (1–3 weeks to library)
2. Apply for TikTok artist account (free; gives Music Tab + verified attribution)
3. Always use library sound when posting
4. If song was used as Original Sound on >1,000 videos before proper distribution, contact DistroKid to merge/claim

## Back-catalog revival strategy (Doechii pattern)

A catalog song breaks when ONE micro-trend latches onto a SPECIFIC 7–15s window that wasn't the original promotional focus. The cleanest case: Doechii's "Anxiety" 2019 demo (not the original release) became viral, was re-released March 2025, hit top 10 Hot 100 with 5 Grammy noms. Other 2024–26 revivals: Connie Francis "Pretty Little Baby" (1962), Surf Curse "Freaks" (2013), Korn catalog spike, Rihanna "Breakin' Dishes". TikTok 2025: 8 of 10 Billboard #1s had a TikTok moment first.

**Per-song revival protocol**:
1. **Audit each song for 4–6 candidate 10-second windows** (not just the intended hook — bridge, ad-libs, isolated vocals)
2. **Test windows in parallel across burners** (different windows on different burners). Algorithm picks; you don't
3. **Rotate songs week-by-week**, not all at once. One lead song / 2-week cycle. 30–50 clip variations on it. Then cycle
4. **When a window starts taking, triple down** on that exact 10-second window with new visual variants. Don't chase a different snippet
5. **Re-register on TikTok** — old tracks may have fragmented sound-page attributions; have DistroKid claim/merge before scaling

## Caption / hashtag strategy

**TikTok**: 1–2 sentences max. 1 emotional keyword + 1 genre keyword. STT from voiceover counts toward classification. Example: `this hits when you remember everything | #emorap`

**Reels**: 2–4 sentences, keyword-rich, written for SEARCH not hashtags. 5 niche hashtags at end (max). On-screen text overlay names the song or feeling.
- Niche hashtag stacks: `#emorap #sadboy #melodic #juicewrld #kidlaroi`
- Avoid: #followforfollow, #music, #explore, #fyp on Instagram, any tag whose IG search shows "recent posts hidden"

**Shorts**: SEO mode. Title + description includes song title, artist name, genre, feeling keyword. Compound-views winner: "10 PM melodic rap song late night feeling".

## Sound-seeding mechanics

Goal for back-catalog: sound adoption (other creators using your track), not just views. Mechanisms:
- End the propagating snippet **suggesting a continuation** — leave a half-line unfinished so creators want to lip-sync to "complete" it
- Use TikTok-for-Artists analytics → find which countries/age clusters are picking up the sound → tailor burner content to those geos
- Repost best fan-creates to main account (positive sound signal → algorithm boosts distribution)
- Engage in comment sections under fan creates AS the artist (converts casual users to repeat creators)
- Seed sound-page niche micro-trends via burner pages first, so when organic creators arrive the format already exists

## Don't-do list

- "Stream my new song on Spotify" hook — single highest-flop opener
- Watermarks on cross-posts — Reels/Shorts both penalize TikTok watermarks; always re-export clean
- Bad mastering for phone speakers — most TikTok plays on internal speakers; if low-end too dominant or vocal sits too low, completion drops. Re-master a "phone-speaker version" of every snippet
- Hook on drop instead of vocal (see vocal-led section)
- Music-only with no human anchor — algorithms now down-rank pure audio-visualizer content; even 0.5s of human face/movement at start re-classifies
- Post clusters <4 hours apart on same account (clips compete with each other)
- Long captions on Reels (hurts reach); long captions on TikTok (truncated)
- Banned/shadowed hashtags (any tag whose search shows "recent posts hidden")
- Botted views or comment manipulation — DistroKid forwards Spotify fraud penalties
- Identical cross-posts (all 3 platforms identify and suppress)

## Workflow cadence (sustainable indie volume)

- **1× weekly studio session (4 hrs)** → 30–50 raw clips (face B-roll, performance, lifestyle, talking-head)
- **2× weekly AI-generation batch (2 hrs each)** → 20–40 AI clips (Veo 3.1 free + Higgsfield Cinema Studio)
- **Daily 30-min editing block** → 4–8 finished clips by mixing one piece of each pool with a chosen 10-second snippet
- **Scheduling**: Buffer/Later for Reels/Shorts, native scheduler for TikTok, cross-posting tool for burner network

Target: **15–40 daily algorithmic shots** across main + burners, sustainable indefinitely.

## Caveats / known unknowns

- The "burner page" playbook lives in TikTok's coordinated-inauthentic-behavior gray zone. Periodic purges. Burners must look genuinely human
- AI generation tools change monthly. Re-audit toolstack quarterly
- Stat citations (e.g., "60% of fraud from social promo", "5–10× reach delta on 3-second hold") are directionally consistent across sources but specific numbers should be treated as estimates, not audited
- Catalog-revival mechanism is preparable, not forceable — you can stack the deck but not force the moment
