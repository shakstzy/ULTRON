# Jeff

Sei (blockchain protocol) contact. Adithya pitches him on **Forge**, a Berkeley student startup accelerator project Adithya is building.

## Forge pitch (April 2026)

> "Forge. Student-run, zero-equity, 501(c)(3) nonprofit startup accelerator at Berkeley. Modeled on Prod (MIT/Harvard) which helped 30+ teams raise $25M+."

Key structural elements Adithya pitches:

- Three tiers: Explore (pre-accelerator), Core cohort 8–14 teams, Alumni.
- Cohort 1: Fall 2026, 14-week single-semester proof of model.
- Zero equity, $10K–$50K credits per team.
- Demo Day in December with 15–20 VCs.
- Student CEO at Berkeley as #1 hire (remote founders run strategy/fundraising at 8–10 hrs/wk).
- Lean budget: $9–$15K Year 1.
- Sponsor tiers: $5K founding, $2.5K gold, $1K silver.
- Hard rule: "No Outerscope investment in Cohort 1 companies, period. Clean firewall."

Tactical plays Adithya identifies:

- CalHacks talent arbitrage (1000+ builders, no follow-up pipeline).
- BAIR undergrad pipeline gap.
- Transfer students (~30% of Berkeley) as the most startup-ready ignored cohort.
- Housing co-ops as recruiting channel.
- Reverse Demo Day (VCs pitch teams first).

Jeff's response: "lmao, yeah ideas are common these days, someones got to do it / are you familiar with Prod? prob less like a program and more just an ad hoc group."
