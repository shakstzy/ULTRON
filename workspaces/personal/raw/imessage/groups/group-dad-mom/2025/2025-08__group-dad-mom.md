🕵️‍♂️ Keep It Private

Don’t announce your fast.

Avoid social pressure or unnecessary questions from friends or coworkers.

Use an Excel sheet, notepad, or app to:

Log start and end times.

Record how you're feeling each day.


Journaling creates accountability and helps track patterns.



---

💧 Hydrate Frequently

Most hunger pangs are actually dehydration.

Drink water regularly — before and during hunger waves.

Staying hydrated reduces false hunger signals.



---

🔥 Aim for Autophagy

Autophagy begins around 36–72 hours of fasting (varies by individual).

This is when your body starts cleansing dead cells and burning fat.

Getting to this phase is a primary health benefit of extended fasting.



---

🧪 Use Keto Sticks for Motivation

Keto sticks help measure ketone levels.

Deep ketosis usually starts after 24–36 hours.

Seeing progress discourages you from breaking the fast prematurely.



---

⚠️ Watch Out for Headaches

Headaches are common after 2–3 days due to electrolyte imbalance.

Avoid additives like:

Apple cider vinegar (ACV)

Lemon (unless sure it doesn’t break your fast or kick you out of ketosis)


The goal is to avoid triggering digestion.



---

🧂 Replenish Electrolytes

Consider adding:

Salt to water

Electrolyte supplements

Pickle juice (some swear by it, but try cautiously)


Helps prevent dizziness, headaches, or fatigue during extended fasts.



---

🎯 Set a Clear Goal

Decide in advance: 3, 5, 7, or 10 days — and commit.

Having a defined goal helps you stay disciplined.

Even doing it once can shift your relationship with food permanently.
