Just went through the SCOR / Sweet materials you forwarded. Here’s how I’m thinking about it.

What this actually is
Sweet is basically a Web3 sports engagement platform that already runs digital collectible programs for big leagues like NHL and MLS.  
SCOR is the rewards / ecosystem token that sits on top of their mini-games (mostly on Telegram right now) where users earn “Gems” in casual arcade-style sports games and later convert those Gems to $SCOR after TGE.  

They’ve now expanded from hockey/football-style games into cricket with Sixer Smash, using recognizable T20 players and tapping into a huge, very online fan base.  

What I like
Real distribution vs pure vapor:
They’re not pitching a whitepaper game – there’s an existing user base in the hundreds of thousands on Telegram and web, with partners/leagues that are actually “real” (NHL, MLS, and now Winners Alliance for cricket).  

Right wedge/narrative:
Sports × casual gaming × Telegram mini-apps is a good wedge. We’ve already seen how fast these mini-apps can grow when they hit (Hamster Kombat, etc.), even if most of those were very low-quality. SCOR is at least trying to anchor that energy to licensed sports IP and a more sustainable economy.  

Multi-chain architecture:
Launching across Solana, TON and BNB using LayerZero’s OFT standard is a sensible design: one global capped supply, burn/mint across chains so you don’t have fragmented wrapped versions everywhere. In theory that gives them access to Solana degen liquidity, TON’s Telegram growth, and BNB / CEX alignment all at once.

Tokenomics at least trying to be sane:
4B total supply with a path to 1B via burns and consumptive use, plus a big chunk of supply reserved for ecosystem incentives.  
The “1/3 burned, 1/3 community rewards, 1/3 back to platform” pattern is the right directionally—it forces spend and sinks instead of pure emissions. It’s better thought through than 90% of game tokens.

Risks / open questions
This is still very much in the high-beta, speculative bucket for me:

GameFi / tap-to-earn decay risk:
We’ve seen this movie: huge early numbers, then engagement falls off once the airdrop/points meta cools down. The update quotes headline KPIs (1.4M players, 600k MAU), but we don’t see cohort retention, ARPU, or what % of that activity is organic vs campaign/airdrop-driven. Without that, it’s hard to underwrite durable value.

Does value actually accrue to $SCOR?
Deflationary mechanics sound nice, but the real question is:
	•	Will users actually spend SCOR at scale (vs farming and dumping)?
	•	Is there a compelling reason for leagues/IP partners to care about the token price beyond marketing?
	•	How much of future upside is going to equity vs the token?

Allocation / FDV dynamics:
From what’s public, 21% of supply is for private sale, 44% for ecosystem, with the rest to team / treasury / partners.  
Depending on what FDV they price the SAFT at and how the vesting is structured, this can very easily turn into a classic “low float, high FDV” trade that performs well at TGE then bleeds as supply unlocks.

Crowded category:
On the sports side they’re competing with things like Sorare and other fantasy/sports crypto products that already have real volume and brand recognition.  
On the Telegram / mini-app side they’re competing with every other tap-to-earn project chasing the same user base and attention. The IP helps, but it’s not an automatic moat.

Execution + regulatory:
They’re clearly trying to frame SCOR as a “consumptive-use” token and have US counsel involved, which is good, but any US-touch token with rewards + secondary market trading still sits in a grey zone. That’s fine for a small speculative position, just not something I’d treat as a low-risk income play.

How I’d frame it for you
If I were doing this out of my own venture book, I’d bucket SCOR as:

“Credible team + real distribution + real IP, but structurally still a high-risk GameFi/consumer token.”

So my view:
	•	If you can get friendly SAFT terms (meaningful discount to implied TGE price, sensible vesting, no crazy FDV), and you’re comfortable treating this as a small, venture-style bet with asymmetric upside, it’s a reasonable punt.
	•	I wouldn’t size this like a core crypto position or rely on the deflation narrative alone. The outcome will live or die on retention and whether they can make SCOR feel genuinely useful across multiple sports games, not just this first batch of mini-apps.
	•	Practically: I’d want to see the exact SAFT terms (valuation, lockups, vesting schedule, and any investor protections). If those are aggressive (high FDV, long lock, no real discount), I’d lean toward waiting for post-TGE price discovery and treating it as “fun money” size only.

Happy to dig into the actual SAFT if you send it over, but that’s my initial read based on what they’ve shared so far
