**16:26 — me:** Some non obvious shit I’ve been thinking about:

The CalHacks Talent Arbitrage — Thousands of builders come through CalHacks every year but there's zero structured follow-up pipeline. They build for 36 hours, win prizes, go back to class. Forge should position as "what happens after the hackathon" and run a post-CalHacks intake funnel.

The BAIR-to-Founder Gap at the undergrad level — Berkeley AI Research produces faculty/PhD founders (Databricks, Anyscale) but there's a massive gap at the undergrad/masters level. Students doing BAIR research have deep technical skills but zero commercial instinct. This is a specific, targetable cohort nobody is serving.

Transfer Student Goldmine — ~30% of Berkeley students are transfers. They're older, more motivated, have zero social network on campus, and can't afford unpaid FAANG internships. They're the most underserved and most startup-ready demographic. No existing org targets them.

The Berkeley Housing Co-op Pipeline — BSC co-ops are inherently communal, countercultural, and builder-minded. Co-op residents already live together and share resources. No startup org is recruiting from co-ops. This is a free, high-quality channel.

The Graduation Cliff — The real kill zone isn't during the program, it's the 6 months after. Teams disintegrate when students scatter ive literally seen it happen so many times bro. V2 adds a specific post-graduation retention mechanism (Bay Area co-working access, alumni stipend program, hacker house aspiration).

The Reverse Demo Day concept — Have VCs pitch their thesis to teams first. Flips the power dynamic, educates founders, and makes VCs feel like they're contributing rather than being sold to. Then do traditional Demo Day at the end.

Sponsor Value Inversion — The real pitch to sponsors isn't "logo placement." It's product feedback. 12 teams building on your platform = 12 beta testers finding bugs and providing real usage data. Position credits as paid R&D, not charity.


Counter-Cyclical Recruiting — Most orgs recruit at the semester involvement fair. By October, recruiting is dead. Forge should recruit in November when students are disillusioned with their fall clubs and questioning their trajectory.


The Anti-Accelerator Social Positioning — Forge's actual moat isn't programming or credits. It's being the place where the coolest builders hang out on Thursday night. Position it as a social scene first, program second.
