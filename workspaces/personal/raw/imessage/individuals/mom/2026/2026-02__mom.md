**14:14 — Mom:** 🌿 Cannabis and ADHD: What Actually Happens?

ADHD already involves challenges with impulse control, attention, and executive function — mainly linked to differences in dopamine signaling and prefrontal cortex regulation. Cannabis interacts with many of the same brain systems.

Here’s how they overlap.


---

🧠 Why It Feels Like It Helps (Short Term)

Some people with ADHD report that cannabis:

Slows racing thoughts

Reduces restlessness

Lowers anxiety

Makes them feel calmer or more focused


That’s because THC can temporarily dampen overstimulation and emotional reactivity. If someone’s ADHD presents with high anxiety or hyperactivity, that calming effect can feel like better control.

But “feeling calmer” ≠ improved executive function.


---

⚠️ What Research Generally Shows

1️⃣ Impulse Control

Cannabis typically reduces inhibitory control, even in people without ADHD.
In ADHD — where impulse regulation is already vulnerable — this can mean:

More risk-taking

More reactive decisions

Harder time delaying gratification



---

2️⃣ Attention & Working Memory

THC impairs:

Working memory

Sustained attention

Task organization


Those are already core ADHD challenges. So cannabis can amplify existing weaknesses, even if it reduces hyperactivity.


---

3️⃣ Dopamine Effects

ADHD involves dopamine regulation differences.
THC affects dopamine release in reward pathways.

Short-term: slight dopamine spike → feels rewarding
Long-term heavy use: blunted dopamine response → can worsen motivation and focus

That’s why frequent use is linked to:

Increased procrastination

Reduced drive

More difficulty with structured tasks



---

📊 Risk of Cannabis Use Disorder

People with ADHD are statistically more likely to:

Start using earlier

Use more frequently

Develop dependence


Impulsivity + reward sensitivity = higher vulnerability.


---

👶 Age Matters a Lot

If cannabis use begins during adolescence (while the prefrontal cortex is still developing), there’s stronger evidence for:

Worsened executive functioning

Longer-term cognitive changes

Greater addiction risk


Adult-onset occasional use appears less risky cognitively, though still impairs performance while intoxicated.


---

💊 What About Using It Instead of Stimulants?

There’s no strong evidence that cannabis treats ADHD symptoms effectively.

In contrast, stimulant medications (like methylphenidate or amphetamines) are well studied and improve:

Inhibitory control

Task persistence

Working memory


Some people reduce stimulant side effects (like anxiety or insomnia) with cannabis — but that’s symptom management, not core ADHD treatment.


---

🧩 The Common Pattern I See Reported

People with ADHD often describe this cycle:

1. Use cannabis → feel calmer, less overwhelmed


2. Executive functioning subtly worsens


3. Productivity drops


4. Stress increases


5. Use again to regulate stress



It can become a self-reinforcing loop.


---

⚖️ Bottom Line

For ADHD:

✅ May temporarily reduce hyperactivity or anxiety

❌ Usually worsens attention, working memory, and impulse control

⚠️ Higher risk of dependence

📉 Heavy long-term use may worsen executive functioning
