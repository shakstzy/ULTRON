**20:21 — Nihar:** Devi Mahatmyam (also called Durga Saptashati or Chandi Path) is one of the most important scriptures in the Shakta tradition of Hinduism, which worships the Divine Mother as the supreme power of the universe. It forms chapters 81–93 of the Markandeya Purana and is composed of 700 verses, hence the name Saptashati (“seven hundred”).

The text glorifies the Supreme Goddess (Devi) as the ultimate cosmic force responsible for creation, preservation, and destruction of the universe. Through a series of mythological narratives, it describes how the Goddess manifests in different forms to defeat powerful demons that threaten cosmic order. The most famous episodes include the slaying of Mahishasura by Durga, the destruction of Shumbha and Nishumbha, and the fierce battle against Raktabija.

Philosophically, the Devi Mahatmyam establishes the Goddess as Adi Shakti, the primordial energy underlying all existence. It emphasizes that the Divine Mother is both compassionate and powerful—protecting devotees while destroying evil. Because of its spiritual significance, the text is widely recited during Navaratri, when devotees worship the nine forms of the Goddess.

Overall, the Devi Mahatmyam is not only a sacred narrative of divine battles but also a profound theological work that celebrates the feminine principle as the ultimate reality and source of all strength, wisdom, and liberation.
