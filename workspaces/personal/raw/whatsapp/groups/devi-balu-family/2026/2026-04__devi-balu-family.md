## 2026-04-20 (Monday)

**15:47 — Mom:** Twenty years ago, Amma returned to her eternal home.

In that sacred moment, while our hearts held on, hers had already dissolved into peace—ready to merge with the Infinite. What we experienced as separation, she experienced as fullness.

The body is transient. The Self is eternal.

The Lord teaches in the Bhagavad Gita:

“ந ஜாயதே ம்ரியதே வா கதாசித்”
— The Self is never born, nor does it ever die. (2.20)

Amma understood this not as philosophy, but as Truth lived.

Thirumoolar sings in Tamil:

“உடம்பினை நீக்கி உயிரை உணர்;
உயிர் உணர்ந்தால் சிவம் உண்டாம்.”

— Release attachment to the body and realize the Self;
when the Self is realized, Śivam alone remains.

In the spirit of the Siddhars:

“பிறப்பும் இல்லை, இறப்பும் இல்லை;
இருப்பது ஒன்றே இறை நிலை.”

— There is no birth, no death;
only the One divine existence is.

Letting Amma go was not separation, but surrender—
offering her into the boundless grace of the Divine,
where suffering ends and only ānanda remains.

“அவள் போகவில்லை…
அவள் சிவத்துடன் கலந்தாள்.”

— She did not go away…
She merged with Śivam itself.

Like water returning to water,
like space dissolving into space,
she became That which has no beginning and no end.

Thirumoolar reminds us:

“சிவமே அனைத்தும்;
சிவமே நான், நீ, எல்லாம்.”

— Śivam is everything;
Śivam is I, you, and all that exists.

Today, as always, I do not remember her as gone.

“அவள் எனக்குள் வாழ்கிறாள்.”
— She lives within me.

I offer my deepest gratitude to you for the quiet kripā that continues to shape my life.

Without my even realizing it at that time, your love and guidance became the doorway that gently turned my heart inward. Your presence, your love, and even your departure all became part of that silent guidance.

Today I see that my movement toward Vedānta was not only a personal seeking, but also a grace that flowed through you.

You did not only raise me in life—you awakened something timeless within me. For this unseen blessing, I remain ever grateful.

With love that continues beyond time,
I remember you not as gone, but as the quiet presence that still leads me inward.

Thank you, Amma, for your kripā that continues to unfold as understanding, inquiry, and peace.

Forever grateful. Always connected.

Om Namaḥ Śivāya
Om Shāntiḥ Shāntiḥ Shāntiḥ!
