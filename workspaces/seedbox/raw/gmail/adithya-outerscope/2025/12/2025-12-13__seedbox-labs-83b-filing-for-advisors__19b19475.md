*What is an 83(b) Election?*

Since your shares are subject to vesting, the IRS normally taxes you on the
value of the shares every time a tranche vests. If the company grows, you
would owe income tax on the increased value each year, even though you
haven't sold the stock.

By filing an 83(b), you elect to be taxed on all the shares *now* rather
than later.

*Why is this beneficial?*

   -

   Massive Tax Savings: Since the current value of the shares is nominal
   (near zero), your tax liability right now is likely negligible (pennies or
   a few dollars).
   -

   Capital Gains: Once filed, future gains are generally treated as capital
   gains (lower tax rate) rather than ordinary income when you eventually sell.
