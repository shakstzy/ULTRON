*The most-used design tool after Figma is now AI*
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C1vfsX_jo5hpDi0lpQs8XeAV/https%3A%2F%2Flink.mail.beehiiv.com%2Fv1%2Fc%2F5uW4IypQlqkK00SlrOkxIp%252FUyFzKwYhdg67%252BmQb3OKEMZGs3WVpVXkvZOlS%252B%250AEQQxBuAeL7W%252Bhmjshjkjj13aOAJJkQPPqxF8RIg%252B%252F48BBCzlf0pBz4BBQdjK%250A3biOI6pDikAJqKWLPV9vG2fejsrU8eA5ZeAeH7favfowf01wvY0wBaY0THKH%250A7imBOesZGUwp8t4yW9TLFESKAwPrOycimw%253D%253D%250A%2F7da7d8280f893bf0>

Five of the ten most-used weekly tools are AI. Claude is at 50.8%, which
makes it the #2 weekly tool in design after Figma. Claude Code sits at #4,
ahead of FigJam.

An AI coding terminal is now more embedded in designer workflows than any
canvas-first tool. That happened quietly.
The profession split into thirds.
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C1vfsX_ZWVWBDWVMggX3p-xP/https%3A%2F%2Flink.mail.beehiiv.com%2Fv1%2Fc%2FEt76omgHNAX%252FxaN8cdLr8al1FIvBr1%252BtScTluPxPJXqPTcL8VUqCVkzvPZpD%250Au55I8swoF0ugLOoNf%252BUaLv0p65ECvPkkvXmy1xEWbVNAhSSUiAHkmC3Gc%252F6a%250AMN59EqkZPyJOr1NUIIrlNjlgf8MTVCxn9N68B262ytshrn0mnB8aDv%252BJS38T%250Ala%252FYYJiKzY1k89Kb3PHVa%252BMRURDKzsjp2Q%253D%253D%250A%2Fd91bf48c8546bf70>

43.8% of designers spend more than half their time vibe coding. 31.1% say
it's most or all of how they build. And 37.7% do zero.

Not "a little." Zero.

These aren't different generations. They work in the same orgs, on the same
products, in the same Slack channels. The gap between design engineers at
80.9% and IC designers at 35.0% is the widest split in the data.
More than half of designers are building for themselves.
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C1vfsX7Xl3u8YcMSNACGx-jQ/https%3A%2F%2Flink.mail.beehiiv.com%2Fv1%2Fc%2FxYKpgYu8aEh5IcEJKqWd6uoYa0t5rYVQDGukIvomh7pwBEirRYaFOzjZL0k0%250Av4ZRa0wm2LtabC7aORt%252Fo83hYbguy42%252B1%252FKv3kbH1MYkCmTvAPHnqv3dRMxi%250AwgZHGa0y8Zadn4xFYEg5JheHD17H6iF%252FwaGELY5SpheFvr%252FoIyiggyagJXFP%250A8f0bL3oHPaWvCz6Cbd8MkweXxBSLQ1vlpA%253D%253D%250A%2F8040192d40e49dc8>

59.1% have built their own tool, app, or utility with AI in the last six
months. One in four does it regularly. Only 10.4% have no plans to.

Two years ago that number was in the low single digits. The ability to
build something for yourself — not a product, just a thing that solves your
own problem — unlocked something.
The biggest blocker isn't AI quality. It's time.
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C1vfsXzOjg-2KM2F4gUhT_lx/https%3A%2F%2Flink.mail.beehiiv.com%2Fv1%2Fc%2FnjV4tuMDlig4JMIJjwZRYn1%252FiUSFs0GJcLWMY4Qyz%252BQ2ImC6GDfyLy3G5pme%250AQjSaaS8VZNzMhxVCmuyYXUsdtdFXdWNYEtLWoLMhr3LrccNqi2Tii9czIOOZ%250ASipVJAOuvFic44wZzBr4rvXuxhKPo%252Fst5mjXQe5cD0gLQ2JJuC8yr%252B2foxS%252F%250A9vr7gufMiMGxxH1nh0RWqsjp2ZGROv9l4w%253D%253D%250A%2F4e9895991d43ef7e>

Time to learn tools (55.7%), too many tools to evaluate (53.0%), and AI
output quality (52.2%) are within 3.5 points of each other. The industry
shipped a hundred tools and forgot to give anyone time to learn them.
Design engineers feel more valuable. Researchers feel most at risk.
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C1vfsX_U1FBhmGRDmgVtrdvL/https%3A%2F%2Flink.mail.beehiiv.com%2Fv1%2Fc%2FHIJTAZDZFG%252Frem5NciXrXiukFreLjV8g%252FPsVpf%252F2U8rLVlng%252BUs6YlRx3CdA%250AVqtEBi33tjJa8I0zfQl%252Bqs1s7JRcUNmFTiiHe%252Bm9tDc4d39HIOmXUH20TLfg%250AT6goxZX9Az%252BtPb4%252B7a0dk1%252BdUmdzqbh%252FwUh2IhFYVoA1weHOTNFxMppTuQPL%250AhENMUq9JYIEXM3NuLk%252FTxhArZkfTykfBjA%253D%253D%250A%2F397bf1fef1a7b9b4>

Design engineers: 50.0% feel more valuable, 10.6% feel less secure.
Researchers: 17.4% feel more valuable, 39.1% feel less secure.

Same industry. Opposite experience. If your org isn't talking about this
openly, it's not because the problem isn't there.
Vibe coders are measurably more satisfied.
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C1vfsX_mpkeqYmb-cQFCP2QA/https%3A%2F%2Flink.mail.beehiiv.com%2Fv1%2Fc%2FzPt81RdTi7ypZfMDYN7Gm6buuzh00JjNl%252FQUxBgaA6Q0yRhz6n4vuH2yWqId%250AkWfdMxleKr41QySIDTU1PEFnLzZgyhnMpPwnR6J3OywB4Bxn7BPqYKLcWlZ9%250AOqdmVM9cmo4Dmu232hxbegLkefBM%252Bd3snII68PoGEk1Qd03iOnKYpR%252Bs%252B4rz%250AuWkLi9OG8wD3b7JhQgzY69FA3hja2BRsgw%253D%253D%250A%2Fee2fcfa66284e9fc>

No vibe coding: 5.93/10. Heavy vibe coders: 7.39/10. The gradient is nearly
linear. Cross-sectional data, so I can't prove causation. But the
correlation is strong enough that dismissing it requires its own
explanation.
