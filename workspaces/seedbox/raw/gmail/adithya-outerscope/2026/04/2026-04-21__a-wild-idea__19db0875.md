Avery,

Great meeting you at DLD, and such a small world.

Here’s a first pass at what I’ve been working on. I’m sure there are open
questions, and you may need more clarity for your engineer to really start
hacking on this, but I wanted to get something in your hands.

For me, this sits as a critical project at a critical moment. As I went
deep on a regenerative research project with a billionaire, it became very
clear that while we can solve a great deal with technology, if we don’t
simultaneously create mechanisms to preserve the world’s most critical
land, much of that progress risks becoming irrelevant.

This is an attempt to address that gap.

Let me know where this feels clear, where it doesn’t, and what you would
need to take it further.

*This is a brief overview of A Wild Idea
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C2tVK9dS0oaObuAfegfMFYSY/https%3A%2F%2Fdocs.google.com%2Fdocument%2Fd%2F15FIeJqibHa06WxkyEpCOLlxkMuygtkFNvO4tsHh0q9g%2Fedit%3Ftab%3Dt.0%2523heading%3Dh.vtqfaljr05zv>
and
the core concept behind the real time WILD IDEA map that needs to be built*

*Relevant existing tools and datasets are included here.

MAPPING PROJECT:

We now have, for the first time, the underlying data to understand, at a
granular level, which parts of every country are essential for life to
thrive.

This includes:


   - Biodiversity
   - Carbon storage
   - Freshwater systems
   - Wildlife corridors

These datasets already exist across open platforms such as Global Safety Net
 and MapBiomas.  See data sets *here.*

The problem is not the data. The problem is that it is fragmented,
inaccessible, and *not actionable or prioritized.*

There is currently no unified, country-level system that brings these
layers together into something clear, visual, and decision-ready so it is
easy to understand and prioritize. *This is as much a design challenge as a
data challenge.*
WHAT WE NEED TO BUILD

A real-time, accessible, country-level mapping platform that identifies:
The most critical 40–50% of land that must be protected to sustain life -
for both humanity and all other species. This is the first step toward a
broader goal:

*A Map to 50/50: *Protecting 40%-50% of the Earth’s surface by 2050 and
showing, clearly, how we get there.
CORE QUESTION

If we were to protect 40%-50% of a country’s landmass: Which specific areas
should we prioritize — and why?

Or more directly: If we were to protect land at scale, where do we focus to
preserve life-support systems?
APPROACH

We integrate five critical ecological layers. Protect mission critical land:


   - Map critical biodiversity areas
   - Map critical carbon storage
   - Map to protect and secure freshwater systems
   - Map critical wildlife corridors and those that need restoration
   - Map and recognize land stewarded by Indigenous communities
   - Include land already under protection

These layers are synthesized into a prioritized, spatially explicit,
visually beautiful, prioritization map that identifies:


   - High-value protection zones - currently protected
   - System-critical land - we must protect
   - Sequencing: what must be protected now vs over time

OUTPUT

For each country, the platform produces a beautifully designed,
easy-to-navigate experience:




*A clear, accessible visual map of the land we must protectA layered
explanation of ecological importanceA practical decision-making toolA clear
prioritization layer*

In future phases, the platform will also enable the purchase of available
land and its integration into a perpetual land trust.
BUILD STRATEGY

Begin with 1–2 pilot countries to validate the model
Expand to 20 countries
Scale to a global, real-time system
WHY THIS MATTERS

We are witnessing a profound contradiction:

While innovation and investment in climate are growing, ecological loss is
accelerating at a far greater pace. We are improving at the margins while
the system itself is unraveling at scale. This project is about shifting
from fragmented effort to system-level clarity and making protection
visible, measurable, and actionable.
THE ASK

I’m looking for a small group of people to help shape this early:

AI and geospatial builders
Systems thinkers
Data integrators
Platform designers

People who know how to take complex data and turn it into something clear,
usable, and catalytic.

This is still early. But it feels like one of those ideas where the data
exists, the need is urgent, AI has made it possible and the missing piece
is design and integration. If this resonates I would love to continue the
conversation.

Warmly,
Lara

Raise initial capital to begin building out the core components.

Mapping Project: Climate Data
Partner with individuals, partners, universities to synthesize existing
datasets (such as Global Safety Net
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C2tVK9Zg4JcC3Ed4Vgguq69E/https%3A%2F%2Fwww.globalsafetynet.app%2Fviewer%2F>,
Mapbiomas
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C2tVK9dd3dWyTPFC-g_NmTmQ/https%3A%2F%2Fbrasil.mapbiomas.org%2Fen%2F%2523>)
and establish a clear framework for identifying priority land for
protection in each country. This would be based on criteria such as
biodiversity, water systems, and nature based corridors, and would serve as
a guide for selecting initial countries for pilot implementation. List of
data, dataset sets and dynamic maps.
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C2tVK9dExEZo3_DwKAAHA5ci/https%3A%2F%2Fdocs.google.com%2Fspreadsheets%2Fd%2F12MOE3bYm3hyDtCxW1yszbww8hi_rf1sioPzAgFpl_3Y%2Fedit%3Fgid%3D0%23gid%3D0>

Legal Frameworks
Partner with leading university law departments to identify existing legal
structures, develop working hypotheses for 20 or more countries, outlining
potential legal structures, including national legacy trusts, that could
support long term land protection.

Technology/ AI Platform
Identify an open source technology partner to scope and design a platform
that enables broad participation, allowing anyone, anywhere to contribute
land to the trust. Platform Concept note.
<https://de7f71ef.streak-link.com/C2tVK-iSkkriWsfv-Aq9UfFB/https%3A%2F%2Fdocs.google.com%2Fdocument%2Fd%2F1zEbGssBVOLUW8heE9rKAF9--FH-5r60fp9kUf2_s-ks%2Fedit%3Ftab%3Dt.0>

Indigenous Rights
Partner with Indigenous leaders, legal scholars, and rights based
organizations to ensure that all models are grounded in principles of
sovereignty, free, prior and informed consent, and long term stewardship.
This work will focus on understanding existing land rights, co designing
governance structures, and ensuring that Indigenous communities are not
only protected, but positioned as central partners and leaders in the plan.

Founding Partners
Begin identifying 5 to 10 high net worth individuals and next gen leaders,
in 2-3 countries, who care deeply about land protection and rewilding, and
who may be interested in joining as founding partners with a level of
ambition comparable to the space race.

*Financial Models *

Develop with key thought leaders new finical mechanisms that generate
revenue for stewartship and restoration of critical land
