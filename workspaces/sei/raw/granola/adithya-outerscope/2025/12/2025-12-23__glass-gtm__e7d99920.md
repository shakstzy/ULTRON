## AI Notes

### Glass Product Vision & Positioning

- Core problem: AI development pipeline lacks cryptographic security and governance

    - Current solutions are patchwork of tools without end-to-end provenance
    - Relies on identity/access management with role-based controls that can be tampered with
- Glass solution: Cryptographic reinforced control and governance for AI systems

    - Treats models, data, tokens, memory as first-class versioned and policy-governed assets
    - Leverages Sei blockchain (fastest Layer 1, highest throughput) + cryptographic expertise
    - Provides verifiable, auditable, immutable tracking across entire AI development lifecycle

### Customer Journey & Market Approach

- Five-stage AI development process identified:

    1. Research & experimentation - iterative model architecture testing
    2. Model building with customer data - bringing in actual training datasets
    3. Pre-production staging - different team manages hardware constraints
    4. Deployment - approval processes and infrastructure management
    5. Inference - ongoing production operations
- Target customer segments:

    - AI labs (OpenAI, Anthropic level)
    - AI startups
    - Enterprise companies building AI applications
- Two potential entry points discussed:

    - Deployment approval process - signed approvals triggering automated actions
    - Content-addressable data store - versioned tracking of models with training data
- Revenue model options:

    1. Platform fee (~$500K/year base) + per-user licensing
    2. Per-GPU management fee ($500/year per GPU in deployment fleet)
    3. Hybrid approach combining both models
