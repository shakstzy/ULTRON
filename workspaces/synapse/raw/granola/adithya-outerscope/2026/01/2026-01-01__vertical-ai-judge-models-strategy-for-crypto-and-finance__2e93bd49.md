# Vertical AI judge models strategy for crypto and finance

> 2026-01-02T01:23:53.422Z · duration 18m 4s · 1 attendees

## Attendees

- **Adithya** (creator) <adithya@outerscope.xyz>

## AI Notes

### Core Product Vision

- Building verticalized LLM-as-a-judge models

    - Cheaper than generic solutions like OpenAI
    - Domain-specific training for better accuracy
    - Initial focus on finance, DeFi, and crypto sectors
- User flow architecture:

    1. Start with domain-specific models
    2. Escalate to tribunal systems (15% better than generic models)
    3. Subject matter expert review for edge cases

### Distribution Strategy & Market Entry

- Network advantages in target sectors:

    - Working with Castlia as potential design partner/advisor
    - Neighbor at 16Z heads AI crypto company (Story Protocol)
    - Contact at BlackRock running evals
- Go-to-market approach:

    - Aggressive design partner acquisition
    - License training data from partners
    - May need to pay partners initially for data access
- Key challenge: Need clear vertical focus before raising funds

### Technical Implementation Challenges

- Training data acquisition bottleneck

    - Manual labeling costs for email datasets
    - Time-limited nature of training data
    - Need continuous updates vs. static datasets
- Competitive moat through proprietary training (similar to Patronus/Glider approach)
- Open source foundation available to leverage

### Leadership & Team Building Concerns

- Recognition of technical leadership gap

    - Need experienced CTO for credibility
    - First 5-10M ARR requires strong technical leadership
    - Difficulty competing for top talent without substantial equity/compensation
- Current role: 50% management, 20% team impact, 30% strategic work
- 2 years eval experience, not core expertise

### Market Positioning & Competitive Landscape

- Closest competitors: BrainTrust, Vellum evaluations

    - Most lack verticalized/specialized judges
- Wedge strategy needed beyond email processing

    - Deliver faster/cheaper value first
    - Extract datasets as secondary benefit
- Market timing risk: Eval landscape changing rapidly
- Need proof of sector expertise before consultant/service trust
