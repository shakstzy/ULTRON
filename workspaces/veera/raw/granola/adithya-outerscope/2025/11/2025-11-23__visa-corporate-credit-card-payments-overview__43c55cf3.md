# Visa corporate credit card payments overview

> 2025-11-23T21:59:48.750Z · 1 attendees

## Attendees

- **Adithya** (creator) <adithya@outerscope.xyz>

## AI Notes

### Corporate Credit Card Payment Flow

- Two main use cases: accounts payable and supplier payments
- Standard payment terms: Net 30/45/60/90 days with early payment discounts (typically 2%)
- Four-party model: Supplier → Acquiring bank → Visa network → Issuing bank → Corporate
- Authorization process validates transaction, clearing happens once daily with net settlement between banks
- Current market: $4 trillion B2B payments annually, only $13.1 billion through corporate cards

### Fee Structure & Economics

- Total interchange fee ~2% split between parties:

    - Issuing bank: 1.25-1.5% (takes credit risk)
    - Visa network: 0.25% (routing/processing)
    - Acquiring bank: 0.25-0.5% (supplier’s bank)
- Supplier typically pays interchange fee (90% of cases) to get faster payment
- Banks create money through fractional reserve lending (10% reserve requirement)
- High-value transactions (>$50k) face resistance due to fee size

### Business Opportunity: Crypto-Based Invoice Factoring

- Traditional invoice factoring: Advance cash to suppliers against confirmed contracts
- Crypto enhancement: Use stablecoin treasury as collateral for multiple transactions

    - Corporate deposits 100k USDT as backing
    - Can stake/earn yield on collateral while backing transactions
    - Programmable clawback mechanisms for risk management
- Target market: Small-medium businesses needing working capital
- Competitive advantage: Lower fees than traditional 2% interchange, faster settlement

### Supply Chain Financing Evolution

- Emerging “buy now, pay later” for B2B transactions
- Acquiring bank pays supplier immediately (97k on 100k invoice)
- Charges corporate according to original terms (Net 90)
- All parties benefit: supplier gets immediate cash, corporate gets extended float
- Risk concentrated on acquiring bank/financing provider
- Opportunity to disrupt with crypto rails and lower fees
